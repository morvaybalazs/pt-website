Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 April 2026

B. Morvay Coaching ("we", "I", "me") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains how I collect, use, and safeguard your personal information when you use this website or my coaching services.

Information I Collect

I may collect the following information when you use this website or engage my services:

  • Contact details: Name, email address, and phone number (when you submit the consultation form or contact me directly)
  • Health and fitness information: Training history, goals, dietary preferences, and relevant medical information (shared during coaching)
  • Usage data: Basic analytics such as pages visited and time on site (collected automatically via hosting provider)

How I Use Your Information

Your information is used solely to:

  • Respond to your consultation request
  • Provide personalised coaching services
  • Communicate with you about your programme
  • Improve the website and services

I will never sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored securely using industry-standard measures. Consultation form submissions are processed through Netlify's secure form handling. Coaching communications are conducted via encrypted messaging platforms.

I retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to provide services or as required by law. You can request deletion of your data at any time.

Third-Party Services

This website uses the following third-party services:

  • Netlify: Website hosting and form processing

  • Pexels: Stock imagery (no personal data collected)

Cookies

This website does not use cookies for tracking or advertising. Basic functional cookies may be set by the hosting provider (Netlify) for site performance.

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data I hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Withdraw consent for data processing
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
